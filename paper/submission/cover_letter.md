# Cover letter (SciPost Physics)

Dear Editors,

I submit "Mode-matched record horizons in reversible dynamics: relaxation, redundancy,
and protected exceptions" for consideration in SciPost Physics.

The manuscript turns a long-qualitative subject — the memory arrow of time — into
measured, mechanistically understood, and falsifiable laws. Its central results: an
operational record horizon that tracks the thermalization time for boundary-scale records
across three exactly reversible substrates (a bit-reversible lattice gas, a hard-disk gas,
Clifford circuits up to 128 qubits, with a Haar-random control); a one-slow-mode account
that reconstructs both threshold crossings with no additional free parameters and predicts
held-out runs; a mode-resolved experiment showing the general law is mode-matched — the
entropy clock is record-independent while horizons span an order of magnitude, so
"record lifetime = thermalization time" is the supremum over relaxing records; and a
mapped failure boundary, including a diagnosed realization in which quenched disorder
spontaneously freezes a record at full fidelity for 48 thermalization times.

I believe the work meets SciPost Physics's expectation of opening a new pathway with
clear multipronged follow-up (frozen-record phase diagrams and their relation to
ergodicity breaking; transport crossovers of mode lifetimes; decoder hierarchies), and of
linking research areas (foundations of the arrow of time, observational entropy, quantum
Darwinism, random-circuit dynamics) in a single quantitative testbed.

In the spirit of SciPost's reproducibility standards, all code, per-run data, and
figure-generating scripts are public at https://github.com/lpaiu-cs/entropy-arrow; the
lattice and stabilizer results are bit-exactly reproducible.

The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere.

Sincerely,
Juneyoung Kim
